# Job Evaluation Framework

When Rocco shares a job description or asks whether a role is a good fit, use this framework to structure the response.

## Required Context

Before evaluating any role, read these files if they have not already been read in this session:

- `personalwiki.md` (full professional background, career trajectory, positioning)
- Resume files in the `\resume` folder (current resume and Microsoft certification transcript)

## Evaluation Structure

### Fit Score: X / 10

Be conservative and realistic in scoring. Rocco prefers honest assessment rather than encouragement.

### Role Archetype

Identify the dominant type of role before evaluating fit. Examples include:

- Consulting / Client Architect
- Product Engineering
- Platform Engineering
- AI Research / Data Science
- Program Management
- Technical Sales / Pre-Sales
- Strategy / Transformation Leadership

If the role mixes archetypes, explain the blend.

### Likely Hiring Persona

Identify who is most likely driving the hire. Examples include:

- Engineering manager building a delivery team
- Consulting practice leader
- Product group engineering leader
- Platform or infrastructure leader
- AI research or ML team lead
- Enterprise architecture leadership
- Technical program leadership

Explain what this hiring persona typically values in candidates.

### Strong Alignment

List concrete reasons the role aligns with Rocco's background. Examples:

- Azure architecture or cloud platform design experience
- Enterprise consulting or transformation leadership
- AI platform, generative AI, or retrieval architecture experience
- Security, governance, or compliance architecture
- Relevant Microsoft certifications
- Experience with enterprise environments or regulated industries

### Potential Gaps or Risks

List concerns or mismatches honestly. Examples:

- Role expects heavy hands-on coding or product engineering
- Required technology stack differs from Rocco's strengths
- Role appears more junior than his experience level
- Role expects deep ML research or model training experience
- Role appears optimized for internal engineering rather than consulting architects

Be direct and realistic rather than encouraging.

### Likely Hiring Manager Interpretation

Explain how Rocco will likely be perceived by the hiring team. Examples:

- Enterprise cloud architect with strong Azure credibility
- Consulting-oriented solution architect
- Strong infrastructure and platform architecture background
- Practical AI platform architect rather than ML researcher
- Experienced transformation advisor

Explain what the hiring team may question during interviews.

### Summary

Provide a short paragraph summarizing the real-world fit and how closely the role aligns with Rocco's experience designing Azure platforms, leading enterprise architecture initiatives, and enabling AI adoption.

### Recommendation

Provide a clear answer about whether the role is worth pursuing. Examples:

- Yes
- Yes with caution
- Probably not worth pursuing

Explain the reasoning briefly.

## Scoring Guidelines

- Be conservative and realistic
- Honest assessment over encouragement
- Do not make claims about career results using percentages (e.g., avoid "improved efficiency by 40%")
- Describe outcomes qualitatively when referencing Rocco's experience

## Additional Tasks This Framework Supports

Beyond fit evaluation, this framework context applies when Rocco asks for help with:

- Positioning resume bullets for specific roles
- Writing cover letters
- Writing follow-up emails after interviews
- Improving resume positioning for a target role
- Analyzing job descriptions
- Understanding hiring risk or perception
- Identifying role archetypes and hiring personas

When performing any of these tasks, reference Rocco's experience, certifications, and architecture background from the personal wiki and resume files.
